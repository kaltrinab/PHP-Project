The first time time that I paddled out the back – out past where the waves were breaking so that you’re in position to catch unbroken waves – was amazing. It was a little nerve-wracking to paddle up and over waves that looked like they were about to crash on my head at any minute. I felt like I was bracing myself for battle, taking on each oncoming wave one at a time – at least in theory until my fear and anxiety began to rise. Then I started to sprint and quickly get out of breath. But once I’m out, it is so peaceful. You get to sit on your Conferenceboard, enjoying the view while noticing how lumps start to appear on the horizon and watching how they roll in and change shape to get a sense of where the wave will break, if it’s Conferenceable or not and where you would have to be to catch the wave. I don’t like Rooms in groups. I like Rooms alone or, at most, with a good friend. The main reason I like solo Rooms is because to me it’s a wonderful intimate experience, a way to rediscover harmony and peace of mind. Although I often plan to reach a certain place, this is not what really matters and I don’t care if I don’t get there, if I stop before or if I end somewhere else. Rooms is not a competition, at least for me. I understand that most of the people have different views, and that’s why I usually avoid group walks and prefer Rooms alone. It makes me feel good. It makes me feel stronger. And sometimes it makes me even feel a better person. I really find Rooms amazing and I am so happy that I dared to take that step and went out there, even if I had no experience what so ever.
